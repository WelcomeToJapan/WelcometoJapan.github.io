Fukushima: More Than Just 3.11

By Welcome to Japan Team | July 25, 2025 | Local Insights

When people hear "Fukushima," they often think of the 2011 disaster, widely referred to as 3.11 in Japan. But there's so much more to this prefecture than that. After living here for years, I want to share why Fukushima is worth a visit—or even a stay.

A Foodie's Delight

Let's start with the food. Japan is a foodie's paradise, and that goes for Fukushima too. While 'meibutsu' like Kitakata ramen are worth a try, it's better to think of Fukushima as a place to discover new favorites. The seafood is fresh, the fruit is famous, and local sake is some of the best in Japan.

Winter Sports Paradise

Now, if you're into winter sports, this place is fantastic. Fukushima, especially around Mount Bandai, has some great ski resorts - I snowboard of course, and its easy to get a day pass and rental for a good price, with plenty of less crowded slopes than in Nagano or Hokkaido.

Sightseeing & Nature

For sightseeing, Aizuwakamatsu is probably the first place to visit. It's got Tsuruga Castle, the traditional Ouchijuku village, and history by the bucket. And if you like nature, Lake Inawashiro, Goshikinuma's colorful ponds, and the Bandai-Azuma Skyline road are must-sees.

Getting Around

And yes, getting around is easiest with a car. It makes all the small temples, onsens, parks, restaurants, and hotels easy to reach. And by renting a car, you can see so much more of Tohoku on your own time.

Fukushima is beautiful, as is all of Tohoku. So venture away from the tourist trail and explore!
