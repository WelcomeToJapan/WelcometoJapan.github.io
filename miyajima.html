Miyajima: Island of Gods & Iconic Views

Home to the famous "floating" torii gate and serene natural beauty.

Welcome to Miyajima Island

A sacred island known for its stunning shrine and friendly wild deer.

Miyajima Island, officially known as Itsukushima, is a small island in Hiroshima Bay, famous for its iconic "floating" torii gate of the Itsukushima Shrine. This UNESCO World Heritage site appears to float on the water at high tide, creating one of Japan's most picturesque and photographed scenes.

Beyond the shrine, Miyajima offers beautiful natural landscapes, hiking trails up Mount Misen, and friendly wild deer that roam freely. It's a perfect day trip or overnight stay from Hiroshima, providing a serene escape and a deep dive into Japanese spirituality and nature.

Must-Visit Attractions

Key sights that make Miyajima an unforgettable destination.

Itsukushima Shrine & Torii Gate

The iconic shrine built over water, famous for its "floating" torii gate.

Mount Misen

The highest peak on Miyajima, offering panoramic views and ancient temples.

Daisho-in Temple

A sprawling and fascinating temple complex with unique statues and halls.
