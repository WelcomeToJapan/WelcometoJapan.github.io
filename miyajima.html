Miyajima: Island of Gods and the Iconic Torii Gate

Discover one of Japan's most scenic spots, a UNESCO World Heritage site.

Welcome to Miyajima

A sacred island renowned for its stunning Itsukushima Shrine and wild deer.

Miyajima, officially known as Itsukushima, is a small island in Hiroshima Bay, famous for its iconic "floating" torii gate of the Itsukushima Shrine. This UNESCO World Heritage site is consistently ranked among Japan's three most scenic views.

Beyond the shrine, Miyajima offers a blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. Wild deer roam freely, Mount Misen provides panoramic views after a scenic ropeway ride or hike, and the island's streets are lined with traditional shops and restaurants serving local delicacies like grilled oysters and maple leaf-shaped cakes (momiji manju).

Savor Local Delicacies

Enjoy Sunset Views

Witness the magical sight of the torii gate at high tide during sunset.
