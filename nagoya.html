Nagoya: Japan's Industrial & Historical Hub

Explore a city blending modern innovation with rich samurai history and unique cuisine.

Welcome to Nagoya

A major city in central Japan, known for its manufacturing prowess and historical significance.

Nagoya, the capital of Aichi Prefecture, is a vital industrial center and the heart of Japan's automotive industry. While often overlooked by tourists, it offers a fascinating blend of historical landmarks, modern attractions, and a distinctive culinary culture.

From the grandeur of Nagoya Castle to the innovative exhibits at the Toyota Commemorative Museum of Industry and Technology, Nagoya provides insights into both Japan's feudal past and its technological future. It's also a great base for exploring nearby attractions in the Chubu region.

Must-Visit Attractions

Key sites that showcase Nagoya's history, industry, and culture.

Nagoya Castle

A magnificent castle with golden shachihoko (tiger-fish) on its roof.

Atsuta Jingu Shrine

One of Japan's most important Shinto shrines, housing a sacred sword.

Osu Kannon

Visit this famous Buddhist temple conveniently close to shopping and excellent nightlife.

Experiences in Nagoya

Indulge in Nagoya's unique cuisine and explore its diverse offerings.

Nagoya's Unique Cuisine

Savor local specialties like hitsumabushi (eel), miso katsu, and tebasaki (chicken wings).

Modern Architecture & Shopping

Explore the futuristic Nagoya Station area and its extensive shopping facilities.

Day Trips from Nagoya

Use Nagoya as a base to visit nearby attractions like Inuyama Castle or Gifu.
