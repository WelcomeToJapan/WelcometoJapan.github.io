Welcome to the Heart of Japan's Mountains

The Japanese Alps offer breathtaking landscapes, traditional culture, and thrilling outdoor activities.

The Japanese Alps are a series of mountain ranges that traverse the main island of Honshu, offering some of Japan's most spectacular natural scenery. Divided into the Northern, Central, and Southern Alps, this region is a paradise for hikers, skiers, and nature lovers.

Beyond the stunning peaks, you'll find charming historic towns like Takayama, traditional farmhouses in Shirakawa-go, and numerous onsen (hot springs) to relax in after a day of exploration. The Japanese Alps provide a refreshing contrast to Japan's bustling cities, showcasing a different, equally captivating side of the country.

Top Destinations in the Japanese Alps

Key places to visit for stunning views and cultural experiences.

Takayama

A beautifully preserved old town with traditional wooden houses and sake breweries.

Shirakawa-go & Gokayama

UNESCO World Heritage villages famous for their unique gassho-zukuri farmhouses.

Kamikochi

A pristine alpine valley offering spectacular hiking trails and natural beauty.

Experiences in the Japanese Alps

Immerse yourself in the natural beauty and traditional culture of the Alps.

Hiking & Trekking

Explore numerous trails from leisurely walks to challenging multi-day treks.

Onsen Relaxation

Unwind in the region's many natural hot springs, often with scenic views.

Winter Sports

Enjoy skiing and snowboarding at resorts like Happo-One in Hakuba.
