Finding Japan in the North - A Guide to Travelling Tohoku

By Welcome to Japan Team | July 28, 2025 | Travel Guide

Look, a trip to Japan of course means a few key places – Tokyo and Kyoto especially. It’s called the Golden Route for a reason, and it’s great, but if you’ve never even heard of Tohoku, this post is for you.

Tohoku: Rent a Car

I’m going to say this right away: rent a car. Seriously. The Shinkansen is fantastic for getting between the big cities like Sendai and Morioka, but it's useless for all the best countryside and hot springs. If you want to see the real Tohoku, rent a car.

A Prefecture-by-Prefecture Guide to Some of What’s Around

Tohoku is huge, so here's a quick rundown of some places I think are worth your time in each prefecture.

Fukushima

I’ve already written a post about my home turf, so I’ll keep it brief. Come for the epic, uncrowded snowboarding around Bandai, the history in Aizu, and the incredible onsen towns.

Miyagi

The most famous spot is Matsushima Bay, one of Japan's "Three Great Views." You can take a cruise, but honestly, just rent a car or a scooter and drive the coastal road for the best views.

Also in the area is Zao mountain. It’s probably Japan's biggest single ski resort, but it's mostly gentle green and blue runs. The real reason to go is for the "snow monsters."

Sendai, the main city, is mostly just your gateway – fly or train in, pick up your rental car, and get out into the real stuff.

Yamagata

First up is Yamadera, the temple on a cliff. The 1,000-step climb is tough but plenty of people do it, and the view from the ancient temple at the top is impressive.

Iwate

Head to Hiraizumi. The whole area is a UNESCO site, and it’s gorgeous. Don't just see the famous Golden Hall; drive around. The temple grounds are sweeping and beautiful.

Akita

I’ve got one tip for Akita: stop at the Michi no Eki (roadside stations). We were driving through the mountains and bought huge boxes of apples and grapes for at or below market price in Tokyo.

Aomori

I haven't explored Aomori properly yet – only had a decade! It’s famous for Nebuta festival, Oirase Gorge, Lake Towada, and Hirosaki Castle. It just proves there's always more to see in Japan.

Tohoku's Summer Festivals

If you are visiting in early August, you’ll find some of Japan's best festivals. Of course, mid-August is Obon, so watch out for crowds then!

  • Aomori Nebuta Matsuri (around Aug 2-7): Massive, glowing warrior floats parade through the city. It’s loud and extremely famous.
  • Akita Kanto Matsuri (around Aug 3-6): Performers balance huge poles of paper lanterns on their foreheads, shoulders, and hips.
  • Sendai Tanabata Matsuri (around Aug 6-8): More chill, but beautiful. The city’s shopping arcades are filled with thousands of giant, colorful paper streamers.

Go Explore

Tohoku isn't as convenient as Tokyo, but the rewards are great. The whole region is huge, and honestly, you could make a vacation out of just one prefecture. So get out there and see what you find!
