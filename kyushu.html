Kyushu: Land of Fire & Hot Springs

Explore dramatic volcanic landscapes, therapeutic onsen, and rich history.

Welcome to Kyushu

Japan's southwesternmost main island, offering a unique blend of nature, history, and vibrant cities.

Kyushu is an island of striking contrasts, from its active volcanoes and abundant hot springs to its lush farmlands and dynamic urban centers. It's a region rich in history, having been a significant gateway for foreign trade and cultural exchange in Japan's past.

Visitors to Kyushu can explore ancient castles, relax in world-renowned onsen towns like Beppu, hike through dramatic volcanic terrain, and savor unique local cuisines. Its warm climate and diverse landscapes make it an appealing destination year-round.

Top Destinations in Kyushu

Key cities and natural wonders to explore on Kyushu island.

Fukuoka

Kyushu's largest city, a modern hub with a vibrant food scene and easy access.

Beppu

One of Japan's most famous onsen resorts, known for its "Hells of Beppu".

Kumamoto

Home to one of Japan's most impressive castles and gateway to Mount Aso.

Experiences in Kyushu

Immerse yourself in Kyushu's unique blend of nature, history, and cuisine.

Onsen Hopping

Explore the diverse hot springs, from traditional baths to mud baths and sand baths.

Volcanic Exploration

Visit active volcanoes like Mount Aso and Sakurajima for dramatic landscapes.

Savor Local Cuisine

Enjoy Hakata Ramen, Motsunabe, and fresh seafood unique to the region.
