Welcome to Kyoto

A city steeped in history, offering a timeless glimpse into Japan's imperial past.

Kyoto, once the imperial capital of Japan for over a thousand years, is a city synonymous with traditional Japanese culture. It is home to thousands of classical Buddhist temples, Shinto shrines, exquisite gardens, and traditional wooden houses.

Despite its modern growth, Kyoto has meticulously preserved its heritage, allowing visitors to experience the elegance of geisha districts, the tranquility of Zen gardens, and the spiritual ambiance of its many sacred sites. It's a city where every corner tells a story of artistry, spirituality, and timeless beauty.

Must-Visit Attractions

Iconic temples, shrines, and districts that define Kyoto's cultural landscape.

Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)

A stunning Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf, set amidst beautiful gardens.

Fushimi Inari-taisha

Famous for its thousands of vibrant orange torii gates winding up a mountain.

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Walk through towering bamboo stalks in this iconic natural landmark.
