Welcome to our itinerary guide! Planning a trip to Japan can feel overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be. We've designed these sample itineraries to serve as a launchpad for your adventure. Use them as they are, or mix and match days to create a custom trip that's perfect for you. Below, you'll find plans for various trip lengths and themes.

The 7-Day First-Timer's Classic

Focus: Tokyo & Kyoto

Best for: Seeing the essential highlights on a tight schedule.

Day 1-3: Tokyo. Dive into the world's biggest metropolis. Explore the neon lights of Shinjuku, the famous Shibuya Crossing, and the traditional charm of Asakusa's Senso-ji Temple. Book a hotel in Shinjuku →

Day 4: Travel to Kyoto. Experience the speed and efficiency of the Shinkansen (bullet train). Get your JR Pass here →

Day 5-6: Kyoto. Step into old Japan. Visit the stunning Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), walk through thousands of red gates at Fushimi Inari, and find peace in the Arashiyama Bamboo Grove.

Day 7: Depart. Easily travel from Kyoto to Kansai International Airport (KIX) or take the Shinkansen back to Tokyo for your flight home.

The 14-Day Golden Route & Beyond

Focus: Tokyo, Hakone, Kyoto, Nara, & Hiroshima

Best for: The comprehensive Japan experience.

Day 1-4: Tokyo. Get your fill of the capital with an extra day for a trip to the Ghibli Museum or exploring trendy neighborhoods like Shimokitazawa.

Day 5: Hakone. Escape to the mountains for stunning views of Mt. Fuji. Stay in a traditional Ryokan and experience a relaxing onsen (hot spring). Find a Ryokan in Hakone →

Day 6-9: Kyoto. Immerse yourself in the cultural capital with plenty of time for temples, gardens, and perhaps a traditional tea ceremony.

Day 10: Nara. Take a day trip to Japan's first capital and make friends with the famous bowing deer in Nara Park.

Day 11-12: Hiroshima & Miyajima. A powerful and moving experience. Visit the Peace Memorial Park and Museum, then take a ferry to the beautiful Miyajima Island to see the iconic "floating" torii gate.

Day 13-14: Travel & Depart. Take a final Shinkansen ride back to your departure city.

The 10-Day Anime & Pop Culture Tour

Focus: Tokyo & Osaka

Best for: Fans of anime, manga, and video games.

Day 1-5: Tokyo. Your anime pilgrimage begins! Hit the arcades and shops of Akihabara, hunt for treasures at Nakano Broadway, and visit the Ghibli Museum (book months in advance!). Check Ghibli ticket services →

Day 6: Travel to Osaka.

Day 7-9: Osaka. Explore the electric Dotonbori district, visit Japan's kitchen, and geek out in Nipponbashi (Den Den Town). A must-do is Universal Studios Japan for Super Nintendo World. Get USJ passes here →

Day 10: Depart. Fly out of Kansai International Airport (KIX), filled with memories and probably too much merchandise.

The 10-Day Tohoku Adventure

Focus: Explore the lesser-known, less busy northern region of Honshu to discover the true Japan.

Best for: Those who want to go off the beaten track, have visited Japan before, or want to experience the gorgeous countryside and epic mountains that Japan offers.
