Hokkaido: Japan's Powder Paradise

Experience world-class skiing, snowboarding, and vibrant après-ski in Hokkaido.

Welcome to a World of Powder

While Japan has a wealth of amazing, lesser known ski resorts (your site-hosting guide swears blind by Tohoku snowboarding, for example), it is true that the premier and most highly-regarded winter sports destination in Japan is Hokkaido, notably Niseko United.

Comprising four interconnected ski resorts, Niseko is a global magnet for winter sports enthusiasts. Located at the base of Mount Niseko-Annupuri, it's renowned for its consistent, deep powder snow, diverse terrain, and international atmosphere.

Beyond the slopes, Niseko offers only a limited range of activities, so don't expect apres ski in the style of Europe or North America, but when you have powder this good, who cares!?

Beyond Niseko, though, Rusutsu and other resorts are less busy, while offering just as consistently fine and deep powder. We'll have a whole page (or pages) dedicated to winter sports in Japan soon enough, so watch this space!

Hokkaido's Top Ski Resorts

A great place to start is the Niseko region, where these resorts are all within relatively easy reach.

Grand Hirafu

The largest and most developed resort within Niseko United.

Niseko Annupuri

Known for its wide, gentle slopes, ideal for families and beginners.

Rusutsu Resort

A large, diverse resort offering excellent skiing and family-friendly activities.

Beyond the Slopes

Activities and experiences to enjoy in Niseko when you're not skiing or snowboarding.

Onsen (Hot Springs)

Relax and rejuvenate in natural hot springs after a day on the mountain.

Dining & Après-Ski

Enjoy a diverse range of restaurants, bars, and lively nightlife.

Snow Activities

Try snowmobiling, snowshoeing, tubing, or dog sledding for more winter fun.
