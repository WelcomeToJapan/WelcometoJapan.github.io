Student Visas in Japan

Your guide to studying abroad and experiencing academic life in Japan.

Embarking on Your Academic Journey in Japan

A student visa is your gateway to pursuing education and immersing yourself in Japanese culture.

Japan is a popular destination for international students, offering world-class education, a rich cultural experience, and opportunities for personal growth. To study in Japan for more than 90 days, you will generally need a student visa (College Student visa).

This visa is typically sponsored by the educational institution you plan to attend, whether it's a language school, university, or vocational college. Understanding the requirements and application process is crucial for a smooth transition to your student life in Japan.

Eligibility & Key Requirements

1. Acceptance from a Japanese Institution

You must first be accepted into an accredited Japanese educational institution (e.g., Japanese language school, university, junior college, vocational school). The institution will act as your sponsor.

2. Proof of Financial Means

You must demonstrate that you have sufficient funds to cover your tuition fees and living expenses throughout your study period in Japan. This can be your own funds, or those of a financial sponsor (e.g., parents).

  • Bank statements, scholarship certificates, or a certificate of deposit are common proofs.

3. Educational Background

You generally need to have completed at least 12 years of formal education (high school equivalent) in your home country. Some programs may require a university degree or higher.

4. Health & Character Requirements

Applicants must be in good health and have no criminal record. You may be required to undergo a medical examination.

Student Visa Application Process

1. Apply to a Japanese School

Identify and apply to your desired Japanese language school, university, or vocational school. Once accepted, they will guide you on the next steps.

2. Obtain Certificate of Eligibility (CoE)

Your chosen educational institution will typically apply for a Certificate of Eligibility (CoE) on your behalf at a regional immigration bureau in Japan. This document certifies that you meet the conditions for a student visa.

  • You will need to provide the school with all necessary documents (academic records, financial statements, passport copy, etc.).
  • Processing time for a CoE can range from 1 to 3 months.

3. Apply for the Visa

Once your CoE is issued, it will be sent to your educational institution, who will then forward it to you. You then take the CoE, your passport, visa application form, and other required documents to the Japanese embassy or consulate in your home country (or country of residence).

  • Visa processing usually takes about 5 working days, but can vary.

4. Enter Japan & Receive Residence Card

Upon entering Japan with your valid visa and CoE, you will typically receive your Residence Card (Zairyu Card) at the airport. This card is your primary form of identification in Japan and allows you to reside in Japan as a student.

Important Considerations for Students

Part-time Work: Student visa holders are generally allowed to work part-time for up to 28 hours per week (and up to 40 hours per week during long breaks) with a "Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted by the Status of Residence" (資格外活動許可, shikakugai katsudō kyoka), which you can apply for after arriving in Japan.

Visa Duration: Student visas are typically issued for 6 months, 1 year, 1 year 3 months, 2 years, or 2 years 3 months, depending on your course length. You can apply for renewal before expiration.

Changing Status: After graduation, many students apply to change their visa status to a work visa if they secure employment in Japan.

For the most accurate and up-to-date information, always consult the official website of the Immigration Services Agency of Japan or your educational institution's international student office.
