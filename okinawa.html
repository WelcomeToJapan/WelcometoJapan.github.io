Okinawa: Japan's Subtropical Island Paradise

Discover pristine beaches, vibrant coral reefs, and a unique island culture.

Welcome to Okinawa

A chain of islands offering a distinct cultural experience and stunning natural beauty.

Okinawa Prefecture, located in the southernmost part of Japan, is a tropical archipelago known for its stunning white-sand beaches, crystal-clear turquoise waters, and vibrant coral reefs. Far removed from the mainland, Okinawa boasts a unique Ryukyuan culture, distinct from that of mainland Japan, with its own language, cuisine, and traditions.

Beyond its idyllic beaches, Okinawa offers a rich history, including numerous WWII sites, and a relaxed island pace of life. It's a perfect destination for diving, snorkeling, marine sports, and simply unwinding in a truly beautiful setting.

Top Destinations in Okinawa

Key islands and areas to explore across the Okinawan archipelago.

Okinawa Main Island

Home to Naha, Shuri Castle, and the Churaumi Aquarium.

Ishigaki Island

A popular hub for exploring the Yaeyama Islands, known for its clear waters.

Miyakojima

Famous for its stunning white beaches and incredibly clear, emerald-green waters.
