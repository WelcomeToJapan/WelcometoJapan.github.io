Yokohama: Bay Views, Culture & Cuisine

Discover a vibrant port city with a rich history and modern attractions.

Welcome to Yokohama

Japan's second-largest city by population, known for its international atmosphere.

Yokohama, a short train ride from Tokyo, offers a refreshing blend of historical charm and modern urban development. As one of Japan's first ports opened to foreign trade, it boasts a unique international flavor, evident in its architecture, cuisine, and diverse neighborhoods.

From the bustling streets of its famous Chinatown to the serene beauty of Sankeien Garden and the futuristic Minato Mirai 21 district, Yokohama provides a wide array of experiences for every visitor.

Must-Visit Attractions

Iconic spots that capture the essence of Yokohama.

Yokohama Chinatown

Japan's largest Chinatown, bustling with shops and restaurants.

Minato Mirai 21

A futuristic waterfront district with skyscrapers, shopping, and entertainment.

Sankeien Garden

A traditional Japanese garden featuring historic buildings and seasonal beauty.

Experiences in Yokohama

Immerse yourself in Yokohama's unique blend of culture and entertainment.

Foodie Exploration

Savor diverse cuisines, especially in Chinatown and the ramen museum.

Waterfront Activities

Enjoy bay cruises, stroll along Yamashita Park, or visit the Red Brick Warehouse.

Art & Museums

Discover various museums, including the CupNoodles Museum and Yokohama Museum of Art.
