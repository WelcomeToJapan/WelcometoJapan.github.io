Find Your Perfect Stay

From traditional inns to futuristic capsules, a guide to accommodation in Japan.

Choosing where to stay is a huge part of your trip! Japan offers a fantastic range of lodging, from deeply traditional experiences to high-tech modern convenience. Here’s how to pick the best fit for your adventure.

Ryokan (Traditional Inns)

A stay at a ryokan is more than just a place to sleep—it's a cultural experience. Expect tatami mat floors, sliding paper doors, and sleeping on comfortable futon bedding. Many include elaborate kaiseki dinners and access to hot spring baths.

  • Best for: Cultural immersion, relaxation, romantic getaways, special occasions.
  • Who it's for: Travelers who want to experience traditional Japanese hospitality.
  • Price: $$$ - $$$$ (Often includes dinner and breakfast)

Hotels

You'll find a wide range of familiar, Western-style hotels in Japan. For a great value, look for "business hotels" like APA Hotel or Dormy Inn. They offer small but efficient rooms, often in great locations.

  • Best for: Comfort, convenience, predictability, and travelers with lots of luggage.
  • Who it's for: Families, business travelers, first-time visitors who want a familiar setup.
  • Price: $$ - $$$$

Hostels & Guesthouses

Japan has a thriving scene of modern, clean, and friendly hostels. They are no longer just for young backpackers. Many offer private rooms in addition to dorms, and are a great way to meet fellow travelers.

  • Best for: Saving money, meeting other travelers, social atmosphere.
  • Who it's for: Solo travelers, backpackers, and budget-conscious groups.
  • Price: $ - $$

Capsule Hotels

An iconic Japanese experience! A capsule hotel provides a small bed-sized pod for sleeping. They are surprisingly comfortable, impeccably clean, and offer shared facilities.

  • Best for: A unique experience, last-minute stays, saving money.
  • Who it's for: Solo travelers who pack light and aren't claustrophobic.
