Shikoku: Japan's Island of Pilgrimage & Serenity

Discover ancient traditions, stunning natural beauty, and a unique island charm.

Welcome to Shikoku

The smallest of Japan's four main islands, renowned for its spiritual pilgrimage and rugged landscapes.

Shikoku is an island of profound natural beauty and deep spiritual significance, most famously known for its 88-temple Buddhist pilgrimage route. This ancient trail winds through picturesque coastal towns, lush forests, and serene mountain landscapes, attracting pilgrims and hikers alike.

Beyond the pilgrimage, Shikoku offers a diverse range of experiences, from the dramatic whirlpools of Naruto to the vine bridges of Iya Valley and the vibrant art islands of the Seto Inland Sea. It's a place where traditional culture thrives, and a slower pace of life allows for true immersion in Japan's serene side.

Top Destinations in Shikoku

Key cities and natural wonders to explore across the island.

Takamatsu

Gateway to the Seto Inland Sea and home to the stunning Ritsurin Garden.

Matsuyama

Known for its original castle, Dogo Onsen, and literary connections.

Iya Valley

A remote, mountainous region famous for its dramatic gorges and vine bridges.
